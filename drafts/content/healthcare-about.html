Healthcare Role Categories (ISCO-08)
222Nursing and Midwifery Professionals
Registered nurses and midwives. University-level qualifications. The single largest vacancy category: approximately 1 in 4 clinical healthcare ads is for a nurse (26.7% of all clinical postings). BA-classified shortage occupation (Engpassberuf) with an average vacancy duration of approximately 246 days.
221Medical Doctors
Generalist and specialist medical practitioners. Fifth-largest group by posting volume. Concentrated in hospitals, mental health and rehabilitation, and medical practices.
226Allied Health Professionals
Physiotherapists, pharmacists, dentists, speech therapists, and dieticians. Degree-level specialists with strong demand in outpatient and rehabilitation settings.
325Health Associate Professionals
Medical assistants (MFA), dental assistants (ZFA), ambulance workers, and physiotherapy technicians. Vocational-track roles forming the operational backbone of clinics and practices.
321Medical and Pharmaceutical Technicians
Radiographers, laboratory technicians, pharmacy technicians, and dental technicians. Vocational-track.
322Nursing and Midwifery Associates
Basic nursing care delivered under supervision. Vocational-track.
532Personal Care Workers
Healthcare assistants and home care workers in residential and community settings. BA-classified shortage occupation with the longest average vacancy duration of any healthcare category: approximately 286 days.
531Child Care Workers
Child care workers and teachers aides in healthcare-adjacent settings.

Data Methodology
Source and Collection
31,128 job postings scraped from StepStone Germany between 31 January and 10 March 2026. Only employers classified under ISIC healthcare sectors (codes 8xxx) were included in the initial extraction.
Clinical vs. Non-Clinical
Of 31,128 total postings, 17,276 (55.5%) are clinical healthcare roles (ISCO-08 groups 22, 32, 53). The remaining 44.5% are non-clinical support roles posted by healthcare employers and are excluded from all analytical views.
Genuine Employers Filter
The Genuine Employers filter removes known job aggregator platforms (for example, meinestadt.de) and out-of-scope intermediaries. With the filter applied, 16,264 postings remain. The filter is enabled by default and is the recommended setting for demand analysis.
Classification
Each posting was classified by occupation (ISCO-08) and employer type (ISIC Rev.4) using the Claude API with manual spot-checking and validation.
On-Site and Visa Signal
Over 97% of clinical healthcare postings specify an on-site work requirement in Germany. Approximately 0.1% mention visa sponsorship (39 of 31,128 postings), confirming a structural gap between market need and formal international recruitment infrastructure -- the gap that GATI addresses.
BA Triangulation: StepStone Data vs. Official Fachkräfteengpassanalyse
All five workforce groups tracked in this dashboard (ISCO 222, 325, 226, 532, 221) are formally classified as Engpassberufe (shortage occupations) by the Bundesagentur für Arbeit (BA) in its 2024 Fachkräfteengpassanalyse. The StepStone demand picture is broadly consistent with BA official supply-demand signals.

Key References
BA Fachkräfteengpassanalyse 2024: 163 shortage occupations identified across 1,236 evaluated professions. All healthcare nursing, elderly care, and medical assistant categories are classified as Engpassberufe. Source: Bundesagentur für Arbeit, Statistik/Arbeitsmarktberichterstattung.

CEDEFOP 2023 Germany Skills Forecast: Nursing and midwifery professionals classified as high-shortage occupations, driven primarily by replacement demand from a retiring workforce rather than sector growth. Source: European Centre for the Development of Vocational Training (cedefop.europa.eu).

BA/Prognos Nursing Projection: A shortfall of up to 520,000 nurses projected by 2030 (Prognos AG, “Pflegelandschaft 2030”, commissioned by vbw – Vereinigung der Bayerischen Wirtschaft, 2012). Current unfilled posts estimated at 150,000 to 300,000. Long-term care demand projected to increase by 37% by 2055 (Destatis / GIZ). Sources: Prognos AG / vbw 2012; BA Press Release 2024-19.

Triple Win Programme: Structured international placement programme operated by BA via ZAV (International Placement Services) in partnership with GIZ, targeting nurses from India (Kerala, Telangana) and the Philippines. More than 8,000 nurses from all Triple Win partner countries have been placed in Germany since 2013, with over 6,000 having arrived; employers cover language training and placement costs.
