What this dashboard covers. Job postings scraped from StepStone Germany for the logistics and drivers labour market: truck and delivery driving, warehouse and freight handling, forklift operation, and passenger transport. Every posting is classified to the ISCO-08 standard at the 3-digit and 4-digit level, geocoded to a federal state, and mapped to a derived employer sector. All charts in the other tabs respond to the header filters.
Logistics Role Categories (ISCO-08)
8332Heavy Truck and Lorry Drivers
Professional drivers (Berufskraftfahrer) with C/CE licences. The dominant occupation in this dataset by a wide margin, mirroring Germany's structural driver shortage of an estimated 70,000–100,000 drivers.
9333Freight Handlers
Loading, unloading, sorting and moving goods in warehouses, hubs and depots. Second-largest group; low formal entry barriers and high seasonal elasticity.
8344Lifting Truck Operators
Forklift and industrial-truck operators (Staplerfahrer). Requires the German forklift licence (Staplerschein), a short certifiable qualification.
8322Car, Taxi and Van Drivers
Van-based delivery and passenger driving with a class-B licence, concentrated in last-mile parcel work.
8331Bus and Tram Drivers
Public and coach transport (class D). Acute shortage occupation in German municipal transport; appears in this dataset inside the Public & Passenger Transport sector.

Methods & Caveats
Source. StepStone.de listings for the logistics & drivers scope. StepStone skews toward formal employers; small owner-driver operations recruiting informally are under-represented.
Classification. Machine classification to ISCO-08; ISCO-4 names follow the official ILO unit-group titles. Roughly one in five scraped postings could not be assigned a confident ISCO-4 code and is excluded from classified counts — the header shows classified volume.
Employer sectors. Derived from company names and posting context by keyword rules; agency postings are assigned to Staffing Agencies & Other regardless of end client.
Job titles. Shown after cleaning: (m/w/d)-style markers and trailing city names removed, common German role words translated. Some German proper nouns remain.
Counts. Figures count postings, not vacancies or hires; one posting can represent several openings, and driver postings are frequently multi-hire.
