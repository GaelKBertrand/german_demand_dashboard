What this tab answers: For the main logistics role groups, this tab maps (1) the Indian qualification and licence a candidate typically holds, (2) the Indian framework governing it, and (3) the German entry pathway — licence conversion, the EU professional-driver qualification, and realistic timelines. The central fact of this corridor: Indian driving licences are not directly convertible for professional use, so licence acquisition in Germany is the gating step, not degree recognition.
Scope and methodology: Covers ISCO 8332 (heavy truck), 8322 (van/last-mile), 8331 (bus), 8344 (forklift), and 9333 (freight handling). Indian framework data: LSC (Logistics Sector Council) QP-NOS codes and NSQF levels; RTO licence classes. German data: Fahrerlaubnisverordnung (FeV) licence conversion rules, Berufskraftfahrer-Qualifikationsgesetz (BKrFQG) for the Code-95 professional qualification, BQ-Portal, and the FEG visa framework. Outcomes vary by state and employer; verify individual cases.

Transferable Skills India → Germany
Indian commercial drivers bring high-mileage experience, difficult-road competence, and schedule discipline that German employers value. The verified gaps are: German traffic law and defensive-driving norms, digital tachograph and Lenkzeiten (driving-time) compliance, load-securing standards (Ladungssicherung, VDI 2700), and warehouse scanning/WMS familiarity for 9333/8344 roles. All four are trainable pre-departure; tachograph and load-securing modules in particular shorten the German licensing phase.
Implications for GATI Programme Design
1. The corridor economics hinge on who finances German licensing (€7k–12k for CE): employer-financed models with retention clauses are the workable structure, and the dominant Freight Forwarding sector in this dataset is the partner pool.
2. Bus driving offers the most institutional demand-side partner (municipal operators) and the clearest precedent pilots.
3. Warehouse and forklift roles are volume markets best used as progression layers toward Fachkraft für Lagerlogistik recognition, not primary visa anchors.
4. The visible staffing-agency share warrants the same contract-level worker-protection safeguards as in hospitality.
