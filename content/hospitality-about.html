What this dashboard covers. Job postings scraped from StepStone Germany for the hospitality and food-services labour market: kitchens, service, hotel front-of-house, bakery and food craft, and the housekeeping and support roles around them. Every posting is classified to the ISCO-08 standard at both the 3-digit minor-group and 4-digit unit-group level, geocoded to a German federal state, and mapped to a derived employer sector. All charts in the other tabs respond to the header filters.
Hospitality Role Categories (ISCO-08)
5131Waiters
Service staff in restaurants, hotels and catering. The single largest occupation in the dataset. Vocational or on-the-job training; strong entry route for internationally mobile workers.
9112Cleaners and Helpers in Offices, Hotels and Other Establishments
Housekeeping and cleaning in hotels and commercial premises. Second-largest group; low formal entry barriers, high turnover, and consistent nationwide demand.
4224Hotel Receptionists
Front-desk and guest-services roles. Requires German plus, in international houses, English; a core occupation for the Hotelfachmann/-frau vocational track.
7512Bakers, Pastry-cooks and Confectionery Makers
The German bakery craft (Bäckerhandwerk). A regulated three-year apprenticeship trade with severe successor shortages, especially outside the large cities.
9412Kitchen Helpers
Dishwashing, preparation and kitchen support. Elementary-level entry point that frequently leads into cook apprenticeships.
5120Cooks
Qualified cooks (Koch/Köchin) across all gastronomy formats. A three-year apprenticeship occupation and an official German shortage occupation (Engpassberuf).
3434Chefs
Head chefs, sous-chefs and kitchen management. Supervisory tier above 5120 with several years of professional experience expected.
1412Restaurant Managers
Operational leadership of restaurants and catering units.

Methods & Caveats
Source. StepStone.de listings scraped for the hospitality & food-services scope; StepStone under-represents very small independent gastronomy that recruits informally, so the sector mix should be read as StepStone's market, not the whole economy.
Classification. Titles and descriptions are machine-classified to ISCO-08. ISCO-4 names follow the official ILO unit-group titles.
Employer sectors. Derived from company names and posting context using keyword rules; postings from recruitment agencies are assigned to Staffing Agencies & Other regardless of the end client's industry. Small businesses without a recognisable keyword fall into Independent Gastronomy & Other.
Job titles. Advertised titles are shown after cleaning: gender markers such as (m/w/d) and trailing city names are removed, and the most common German role words are translated. A small share of titles retains German proper nouns.
Counts. Every figure counts postings, not vacancies or hires. One posting can represent several openings.
