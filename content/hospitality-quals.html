What this tab answers: For the main hospitality role groups in this dashboard, this tab maps (1) the Indian qualification a candidate typically holds, (2) the Indian framework governing it, and (3) what German market entry looks like in practice — whether the trade is regulated, what employers actually require, and the realistic language bar. Unlike healthcare, most hospitality occupations are not regulated professions in Germany, so formal recognition (Berufsanerkennung) is usually optional — but §16d/§18a visa routes and employer expectations still reward a recognised vocational qualification.
Scope and methodology: Covers ISCO 5120/3434 (cooks & chefs), 5131 (waiters), 4224 (hotel reception), 7512 (bakery craft), and 9112/9412 (housekeeping & kitchen support). Indian framework data: NSQF levels from NCVET/NQR, THSC (Tourism & Hospitality Skill Council) QP-NOS codes, NCHMCT and IHM curricula, and craft ITI trades. German data: BQ-Portal, anerkennung-in-deutschland.de, and the Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz (FEG) visa framework. Outcomes are indicative and vary by employer and state.

Transferable Skills India → Germany
Indian hospitality training (IHM system, THSC QPs, hotel-chain academies) transfers well on food production, HACCP-style hygiene discipline, and service sequence. The recurring gaps German employers cite are German-language guest interaction, wine and beverage knowledge for service roles, German bakery products and Mehlkunde for 7512, and familiarity with the dual-apprenticeship workplace culture (independent working, documentation). Pre-departure modules targeting exactly these four gaps materially improve retention.
Implications for GATI Programme Design
1. Cooks and bakers are the strongest visa anchors: shortage-listed, apprenticeship-benchmarked, moderate language bar.
2. Service and reception roles are language-led: B1/B2 German embedded upstream is the gating investment, not technical skills.
3. The large staffing-agency share visible in the Top Tiers tab means agency partnerships are a realistic demand-aggregation channel, but worker-protection safeguards must be contract-level.
4. Housekeeping volume should be read as a progression market, not a primary placement market, given visa constraints on elementary occupations.
